About Me

My name is Nathaniel Stokes—though you can call me Nathan, Nate, or whatever feels right. I've been interested in computers ever since my grandfather introduced me to my first logic puzzle. I started building computers at age 11, but it wasn't until junior year of high school, during an actual Java class, that I really found my love for programming. When I fell into the trap that is logic-based languages, I fell hard—I would spend hours after school expanding on basic assignments just for the thrill—and the challenge—of discovery within it.

Still, I was hesitant to turn my hobby into a career. I watched my grandfather burn out when his passion for quality and improvement became reduced to feeding errors into a machine during his professional tenure at IBM. He told me it drained the excitement of discovery and killed his joy—and love—for the field. I feared the same would happen to me: that I'd convert a passion into just another corporate 9-to-5. But after reflection, and in less-than-ideal circumstances, I came to feel strongly that I shouldn't “bury my talent.” I believe it's my responsibility to use the gifts I've been given to grow and provide for my family. A passion shouldn't be hidden—it should be cultivated and used to uplift the lives entrusted to our care.

I have three wonderful children. Jonah, age five, is curious, excitable, and stubborn. Pearl, his four-year-old sister, is determined, clever, and creative. Finally, there's Henry—two years old, fiercely independent, and best described as unbridled chaos. We live in southwest Missouri, just a stone's throw from Oklahoma and Arkansas. I volunteer as a temple cleaner on Friday evenings at the Bentonville Arkansas Temple, which was built just a few years ago. Our home on Indian Creek means outdoor adventures are a regular part of life, and I love watching my kids pour their whole selves into every activity. My family and my faith are the pillars of my life—my wife and kids, my fellow brothers and sisters, and my Heavenly Father are my foundation.
